Welcome to the East River Collaborative interactive flood map. This tool allows you to view and compare the impacts of different flooding scenarios in the East River watershed.


Disclaimer:

Due to a lack of calibration data, this map should be used strictly as a planning reference tool and not for navigation, permitting, or other legal purposes.



Background: The East River flows north from Calumet County for over 40 miles through Brown County and 13 municipalities before entering the Fox River, which empties into the Bay of Green Bay on Lake Michigan. This map shows the scale of potential flooding at various precipitation and Lake Michigan water level scenarios based on data from an East River flood modeling study led by the University of Wisconsin-Madison Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering. Stream gauges were placed along the East River and its tributaries in 2020 and 2021 to collect hydrologic and hydraulic data. Flood scenarios were analyzed using the HEC-HMS and one-dimensional HEC-RAS models and informed by the river flow data and 2010 Brown County LiDAR.



How to Use this Map



Select a Display:

Extent displays the inundation area of the flood event while Depth displays the flood depth in feet.



Select a Lake Level:

The extent and depth of flooding depends in part on Lake Michigan water levels. High shows flooding based on high water levels (583.8 ft IGLD 85); Low shows flooding based on low water levels (575.7 ft IGLD 85). Compare enables a swipe tool to compare flooding between the high and low lake levels. These two water levels are the record high and low daily average levels at the Green Bay NOAA CO-OPS water level station 9087079.



Select Rainfall Amount:

Select one or more rainfall amounts to display the flooding that results from that rainfall and the chosen lake level. 2.1” in 24 hours has a 99.9% chance of occurrence at least once per year (1-year storm). 3.4” in 24 hours has a 10% annual chance occurrence (10-year storm). 5.4” in 24 hours has a 1% annual chance occurrence (100-year storm). 7.2” in 24 hours has a 0.2% annual chance occurrence (500-year storm). Rainfall statistics are based on NOAA Atlas 14.



Add Overlays:

You are also able to add supplementary data to the map.



The Impacted Buildings datasets show which buildings may be damaged during a flood. The Impacted Buildings layers were developed by the UW-Madison Space Science and Engineering Center using FEMA’s Hazus software.



The Social Vulnerability Index (SVI) indicates the areas which may need the most support to cope with the effects of flooding due to the social, economic, and demographic characteristics of the area. Wisconsin Sea Grant created this SVI following methodology of the Center for Disease Control and the University of South Carolina’s Hazards and Vulnerability Research Institute. The SVI is computed at the census block group level using data from the U.S. Census and the Wisconsin Statewide Parcel Map.
